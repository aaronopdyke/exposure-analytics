Methodology

This site compares seven building-exposure datasets for a target country on a single map, aggregated to World Bank Official Boundaries Admin-1 units. The methodology below is country-agnostic; where concrete numbers appear they come from the current build (Morocco) and are labelled as such. Two are top-down models (GAR15, GIRI), two are releases of a bottom-up engineering exposure model (GEM v2023.1.1 and v2026.0.0), and three are building-by-building maps (Overture, Global Building Atlas, Microsoft). They answer subtly different questions, so differences between them are expected and informative — and the vintage analysis below separates differences that are just data age from differences that are fundamental.

Price-year alignment (constant US$)

The models publish USD of different base years, so every monetary figure on this site is converted to constant latest-year US$ with the World Bank WDI US GDP deflator (NY.GDP.DEFL.ZS, country USA; target year and per-model factors are recorded per country in data/<ISO>/meta.json):

Nominal (native-year) columns are retained in the published GeoJSON alongside the _adj constant-dollar columns. A US deflator is used because all sources are expressed in USD; no currency conversion is involved.

Does data age explain the gaps? (vintage analysis)

Even in constant dollars the datasets describe the building stock of different years — GAR15's inputs are ~2011, GIRI's ~2020, GBA's ~2023, GEM v2026's 2025. The explorer's "Vintage adjustment" toggle (and the deck's vintage slides) grow each dataset from its own data vintage to the latest year, using two data-driven rates instead of a guessed constant:

MetricGrowth basis (computed per country)Rate (current build: Morocco)
Value (USD) WB Wealth Accounts produced capital (NW.PCA.TO, real chained 2019 US$) — the same CWON series GAR15 and GIRI are derived from, so growing GAR15 along it and comparing to GIRI isolates pure method differences. Values follow the actual stock path 1995–2020 and extrapolate at the trailing-10-year CAGR beyond it. ≈5.7%/yr (trailing decade)
Floor area (m²) GHSL Built-up Volume growth (2010→2020) — the closest physical proxy for floor space. Real capital grows much faster than floor area because it includes quality upgrading and capital deepening, so the two metrics deliberately use different rates. ≈1.5%/yr

Why toggle it? To test whether a gap between two models is just data age. By default the site shows each model's own base-year values; switching to "grown" answers "what would this dataset say about today's stock if its method were left unchanged?". The result: age adjustment does not collapse the differences — GAR15 grown from 2011 along its own source series overshoots GIRI, and both produced-capital models stay well above GEM's engineering replacement costs. The gaps are mostly method and valuation basis, not vintage. The exception is GEM v2023 → v2026, where stock growth plus the 2024–25 cost re-basing explains most of the change. Models whose data already reflects the latest year (GEM v2026, Overture) have a growth factor of ×1.00 and do not move.

Sanity check — does the value/floor wedge match GEM's own repricing? The two rates imply the stock's average real value per m² rises ≈4.2%/yr (1.057 / 1.015). GEM's releases test this independently (numbers from the current build, Morocco): implied national unit cost (replacement value ÷ floor area) went from $244/m² (2021 US$, v2023) to $320/m² (2024 US$, v2026) — +31% nominal, or ≈4.8%/yr in real terms after the US deflator (×1.138 over 2021→2024). So GEM's own repricing slightly exceeds the wedge, and the 5.7%/yr value path is if anything mildly conservative. (Caveat: 2021–2024 spans the post-COVID construction-cost spike, so the long-run wedge is likely somewhat smaller; "real" is net of general inflation, so the wedge includes construction costs outpacing the deflator as well as quality/composition upgrading.)

Estimated replacement values for the footprint datasets (GEM UCC)

Overture, GBA and Microsoft map buildings but carry no monetary value. Their "value" layers here are estimated replacement values:

estimated replacement value = floor area × ADM1 unit construction cost (UCC)

UCC (USD/m², 2024 US$) comes from the UCC-database compilation of the GEM exposure model — BLDG_REPL_COST_USD / TOTAL_AREA_SQM per GEM admin-1, all occupancies combined (current build: Morocco ranges $292–329/m²). It excludes contents, consistent with GAR15/GIRI. GEM admin-1 UCCs are mapped to WB units via the region matching below; Western Sahara uses the area-weighted UCC of GEM's two southern regions.

Floor area and storey estimation

GEM reports floor area directly. For the building maps it is derived:

  • GBA has modelled heights for nearly every building: floor area = footprint × storeys via a three-tier storey height (<18 m: 3.0 m, <60 m: 3.25 m, else 3.5 m per storey); missing heights count as one storey.
  • Overture and Microsoft are effectively footprint-only datasets in the current build (Morocco) — Overture's num_floors/height are rarely populated and Microsoft's height field is −1 everywhere. Where attributes exist they are used; for the remainder, storeys are estimated with a GBA-calibrated multiplier: the GBA floor-area/footprint ratio computed per ADM1 unit and urban/rural class, applied to each dataset's unattributed footprint. This replaces the earlier 1-storey assumption with the local storey profile of the one source that has heights.

How sensitive are totals to the storey rules? The build recomputes the footprint models under four alternative rules — integer storeys rounded down (min 1), rounded up, and the storey divisor shifted ±0.25 m — and records the envelope in each country's meta.json (sensitivity block). Current build (Morocco): the extreme rules move footprint-model totals by roughly −8% (integer storeys rounded down) to +29% (rounded up), with the divisor shifts contributing ±8–9% — far less than the ~2.7× spread between exposure models, so the storey convention does not change which models sit high or low.

Boundaries: one display standard

All comparisons are displayed on WB Official Boundaries Admin-1. The following applies to builds that include Morocco: WB draws Morocco ending at 27.67°N and carries Western Sahara only as an ADM0 polygon (ISO ESH, "non-determined legal status area") with no ADM1 subdivisions. Maps here therefore show Western Sahara as one whole, hatched unit (ESH000) appended to the Morocco display — never subdivided and never merged into a member state. A consequence worth knowing: WB retains a small sliver of Laâyoune-Sakia al Hamra north of 27.67°N (MAR007), which is why that unit shows very small totals in every dataset.

Reconciling ADM1 where boundary sets differ

The models do not share an admin-1 geography, so each is brought onto the WB units with a method suited to its native form:

ModelNative geographyReconciliation to WB ADM1
GAR15Grid points, per-country files Point-in-polygon; points whose centres fall just offshore (1 km coastal cells) recovered by a bounded nearest join (≤0.1°). The separate ESH file supplies ESH000. Assignment loss ≈ 0%.
GIRIGlobal 5 km raster Each cell assigned wholly to the WB unit it overlaps most (largest-intersection rule) — keeps coastal cells, avoids double counting, and deliberately has no nearest-neighbour fallback so cells from Algeria/Spain/the Canaries cannot leak in.
GEM (both releases)Own ADM1: the 12 official Moroccan regions, including the far south A curated region-matching table maps each GEM region to its WB counterpart — by GEM's region codes for v2026 (ISO-3166-2 style, MA-01…MA-12) and by normalised region names for v2023 (whose codes are GADM-style). Ten regions map 1:1; GEM's two southern regions (Laâyoune-Sakia El Hamra, Dakhla-Oued Ed-Dahab) lie almost wholly south of the WB 27.67°N line and are assigned to ESH000. By construction the WB MAR007 sliver shows no GEM value.
Overture / GBA / MicrosoftIndividual buildings Building centroid-in-polygon, evaluated inside DuckDB against the full-resolution WB polygons.

Urban / rural classification

What "urban/rural exists for residential only" means for GEM v2026: GEM publishes its summary rows as ADM1 × occupancy. For residential buildings each row is tagged URBAN or RURAL; for commercial and industrial buildings GEM publishes only totals with no settlement tag. So a "GEM urban" figure on this site is the urban share of GEM's residential stock only (commercial/industrial value still appears in totals and in the res/com/ind breakdown chart). The v2023 release publishes no settlement split at all.

ModelHow the split is obtained
GAR15Native: urban (tot_cu) and rural (tot_cr) capital columns, all sectors.
GEM v2026Native but residential only (see above); v2023 has none.
Overture, GBA, MicrosoftAssigned by this project: each building's 1 km Mollweide grid cell is looked up in GHSL Degree of Urbanisation (DEGURBA) E2020, R2025A; classes 21/22/23/30 (suburban through urban centre) count as urban, classes 11–13 (and water) as rural.
GIRINot split — GIRI publishes residential / non-residential layers instead, shown as sub-layers in single-model mode and in the breakdown chart.

Comparability caveats

  • Value definitions differ: GAR15/GIRI are the capital stock of buildings downscaled from national produced capital; GEM is engineering replacement cost; Overture/GBA/Microsoft are estimated replacement values (floor area × UCC). None equals market or insured value. All exclude contents — so figures here are lower than GEM's own published country totals, which include a contents column (current build: GEM v2026 Morocco = $271B structures shown here vs $323B incl. $52B contents on GEM's site).
  • Vintages differ even after price-year alignment — see the vintage analysis above for how much that explains (little, between methods; most, between GEM releases).
  • Completeness differs fundamentally: the footprint datasets count mapped buildings; the models distribute national aggregates, so they include what mapping misses (and vice versa).
  • All zonal work uses centroid / largest-overlap assignment as above; totals exclude features outside the WB coastline generalisation.
  • Access: the site is open — the explorer sits behind a simple landing page, and the files under data/ and assets/ (aggregated ADM1 statistics from public datasets) are fetchable by direct URL.
